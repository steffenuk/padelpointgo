Frequently asked questions

How do I score a match?

From the menu, choose Match (sets and games) or Points / Games (continuous or target counting), pick a quick-start preset or configure your own, then tap Start. During play, tap your side of the screen to add a point — or use a remote.

What scoring formats are supported?
  • Real padel scoring (0/15/30/40) with golden point or advantage
  • Best of 1, 3 or 5 sets, with tiebreaks and an optional match tiebreak in the final set
  • Custom games per set, games to win, or a custom starting score
  • Points modes: 21, 24 or 32 points with 2 or 4 serves per player, plus continuous points and games
How do I pair a Flic button?

Go to Settings → Remote → Flic button → Pair button… and hold the Flic button down until it's detected. Once paired: one press scores for your team, two presses for the opponents, and hold to undo. Use Test remotes in Settings to confirm each press is detected.

Can I score from my Apple Watch?

Yes. Install the PadelPointGo watch app, keep your iPhone nearby, and control the score directly from your wrist. Match data syncs between your paired devices.

Can I use my phone's volume buttons or a Bluetooth remote?

Yes — enable the volume remote under Settings → Remote. You can use the phone's volume buttons or a Bluetooth volume remote. Note: while the volume remote is active during a match, the volume buttons control scoring rather than audio volume — adjust speech volume with the sliders under Speech in Settings.

Why does the app ask for Health access?

On Apple Watch, PadelPointGo can start a workout session so your activity is recorded and the screen stays awake while you play. The finished match is saved to Apple Health as a workout. Your health data stays on your device — it's never sent to the developer or any third party. You can revoke access anytime under Settings → Privacy & Security → Health.

How do I read the spoken score / change the speech language?

Turn on Read the score aloud and Announce new server under Settings → Speech. You can set a separate speech language there, so you can score in one language and listen in another.

Where can I see past matches?

Tap the history icon on the main screen. Each finished match is logged with its key events. You can clear the list at any time.

What languages does the app support?

English, Danish, German, Spanish, French and Swedish. By default the app follows your system language; you can override it under Settings → App language (a restart applies the change).

Do I need an account?

No. There's no account or sign-up, and your data stays on your device.
